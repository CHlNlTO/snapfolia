Balete

Also known as: Strangler Fig

General Information

  • Balete (Ficus spp.) is a group of fig trees in the family Moraceae.
  • These trees are known for their unique growth habit, often enveloping other trees.
  • Balete trees play a significant role in local folklore and traditional medicine.

Botany

Balete trees are large with spreading branches and numerous aerial roots. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers and fruit that are a food source for wildlife.

Distribution

  • Found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
  • Common in the Philippines, India, and Southeast Asia.
  • Grows in forests and open areas.
