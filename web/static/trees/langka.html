Langka

Also known as: Jackfruit

General Information

  • Langka (Artocarpus brasiliensis) is a tropical tree in the family Moraceae.
  • It is known for its large, edible fruit, which is the largest tree-borne fruit in the world.
  • The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and for making musical instruments.

Botany

Langka is a medium to large tree with leathery, dark green leaves. The fruit is large, spiky, and green when unripe, turning yellow as it ripens. The flesh is sweet and aromatic.

Distribution

  • Found in tropical regions worldwide.
  • Common in Southeast Asia, including the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia.
  • Grows in forests, gardens, and farms.
